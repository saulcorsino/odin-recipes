Yaroa

Description

is a Dominican Street Food that originated from Santiago. It is made from French fries or a mash of a root vegetable or tuber such as plantain or yuca, then layered with meat and then cheese. Condiments such as mayonnaise, ketchup, and mustard are also often added on top. Beef, chicken, or a combination of the meats is a common part of the dish.

Ingredients

  • 1lb ground Beef
  • 1red onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4oz tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp oregano
  • 1 tbsp adobo(adobo is a dominican seazoning)
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 4oz mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 4oz cheddar cheese, shredded
  • Fries
  • Mayo
  • Ketchup
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preparation:

  1. In a pan over medium heat, add the oil.
  2. Once the oil is ready, add the bell peppers and onions and sauté until soft, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about one minute.
  4. Add the beef and break apart with a wooden spoon or spatula.
  5. Cook until brown, stirring occasionally, about 30 minutes.
  6. Drain the excess oil from the beef. Add it back to the pan and add the the tomato sauce and seasonings.
  7. Mix thoroughly and allow to simmer for 3-5 minutes. Set aside.
  8. Prepare the fries according to package instructions. Once golden and crispy, get a plate and get ready to layer!
  9. Add the fries and a heaping pile of the ground beef mixture.
  10. Next add the cheese and finally, top with mayo and ketchup.
